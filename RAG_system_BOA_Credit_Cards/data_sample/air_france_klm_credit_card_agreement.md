## How We Will Calculate Your Balance

**Purchases:** We will use a method called "average daily balance (including new Purchases)." See the section titled Balances Subject to Interest Rate in your Credit Card Agreement for more details.

**Balance Transfers and Cash Advances:** We will use an Average Balance Method (including new Balance Transfers and new Cash Advances). This balance is calculated by adding the outstanding balance (including new Balance Transfers and new Cash Advances and deducting payments and credits) for each day in the current billing cycle, together with the balances for each day in the previous billing cycle for Balance Transfers and Cash Advances with transaction dates in the previous cycle and posting dates in the current cycle, and then dividing by the number of days in the current billing cycle.

## Interest and Fees

### Interest Rates — Annual Percentage Rates (APRs)

The APR corresponds to the Daily Periodic Rate (DPR): APR = DPR × 365; DPR = APR ÷ 365. Interest charges are calculated using the DPR. Variable rates are calculated by adding together an index and a margin.

**Promotional or Introductory Offers** are temporary APRs or fees offered on qualifying new transactions for a specified period. Each offer is assigned a unique Offer ID which appears on your credit card statement after the first qualified transaction for that offer.

**Current Rates:** See the Pricing Information table above for current rates.

**Rates for Protected Balances:** When an interest rate change for new transactions is applied to your account, any existing balances of that type will be identified as Protected Balances on your statement. These Protected Balances are generally kept at their current APR until the balances are paid in full.

### Penalty APR and When It Applies

The Penalty APR may be applied to new Purchases, Balance Transfers, and Cash Advances for certain default occurrences. We may increase the APRs on new transactions up to the Penalty APR each time a Total Minimum Payment Due is not received by its Payment Due Date. We will provide a minimum of 45 days advance notice. An increased Penalty APR will remain in effect indefinitely.

- Penalty APRs are variable rates with a margin of up to 26.74 percentage points
- Currently results in an APR of 33.49% (0.091753% DPR)
- The calculated Penalty APR will not exceed 29.99%

### How to Calculate Variable Rates

Variable Rates are calculated by adding together an index and a margin. The index is the highest U.S. Prime Rate as published in the "Money Rates" section of The Wall Street Journal on the last publication day of each month. An increase or decrease in the index will cause a corresponding change in your variable rates on the first day of your billing cycle that begins in the same month.

### Calculation and Billing of Interest Charges

Interest is calculated by multiplying each Balance Subject to Interest Rate by its applicable DPR, then multiplying that result by the number of days in the billing cycle. Interest is calculated and posted on the last day of the billing cycle after all other transactions have posted and payments have been allocated.

### Billing Cycle

A "billing cycle" is a time period that ends on a Statement Closing Date determined by us and begins on the day after the Closing Date of the previous billing cycle. Each monthly statement reflects a single billing cycle.

### Paying Interest

Interest accrues and compounds daily on new transactions and balances remaining from previous billing cycles. We will not charge you any interest on Purchases if you always pay your entire "Grace Period Balance" by the Payment Due Date.

- If you have **no active Custom Pay Plan**: your Grace Period Balance = New Balance Total (Statement Balance)
- If you have an **active Custom Pay Plan**: your Grace Period Balance = Interest Saving Balance (New Balance Total minus Custom Pay Plan balances plus Custom Pay Plan Payments due)

We will begin accruing interest on Balance Transfers and Cash Advances on the transaction date. Your Payment Due Date will be at least 25 days from your statement Closing Date.

## Balances Subject to Interest Rate

**Average Daily Balance Method (including new Purchases):** Calculated by: (1) calculating a daily balance for each day in the current billing cycle; (2) adding all daily balances together; (3) dividing by the number of days in the current billing cycle.

**Average Balance Method (including new Balance Transfers and new Cash Advances):** Calculated by: (1) calculating a daily balance for each day in the current billing cycle; (2) calculating a daily balance for each Pre-Cycle balance day; (3) adding all daily balances together; (4) dividing by the number of days in the current billing cycle.

## Payments and Default

### Your Promise to Pay

You promise to pay us all amounts of credit you obtain (Purchases, Balance Transfers, Cash Advances) as well as all interest charges, fees, and other transactions charged to your account.

### Payments on Your Account

You must pay at least the Total Minimum Payment Due shown on your monthly statement by its Payment Due Date. You may pay the entire amount you owe at any time. We will reject payments not drawn in U.S. dollars or drawn on a financial institution outside of the United States.

### Total Minimum Payment Due

The Total Minimum Payment Due = all past due amounts + the Current Payment.

The Current Payment includes four amounts:
1. 1.00% of your New Balance Total (excluding new interest charges, Custom Pay Plan balances, and new Late Fee)
2. New interest charges
3. Any new Late Fee (sum of 1+2+3 rounded down to nearest dollar, not less than $35.00)
4. Any Custom Pay Plan Payment due

### How We Allocate Your Payments

1. First: amount equal to the Total Minimum Payment Due → applied to any Custom Pay Plan Payment due, then to lowest APR balances
2. Excess above Total Minimum Payment Due → applied to higher APR balances first, then lower APR balances, then Custom Pay Plan balances

### Payment Timing

- Mailed payments credited as of the date received if received by 5 p.m. local time at the remittance address
- Payments received after 5 p.m. are credited the next day
- Other payments may be delayed up to five days

### Default

You will be in default if:
1. You fail to make any required Total Minimum Payment Due by its Payment Due Date
2. Your total outstanding balance exceeds your Total Credit Line
3. Your Bank Cash Advance balance exceeds your Cash Credit Line
4. You fail to abide by any other term of this Agreement

If in default, we can require immediate payment of your total outstanding balance and, unless prohibited by applicable law, require you to pay collection costs and reasonable attorneys' fees.
